**Stoicism**, a school of thought that flourished in [Greek](https://www.britannica.com/place/ancient-Greece) and [Roman](https://www.britannica.com/place/Roman-Empire) history of [Classical antiquity](https://www.britannica.com/event/Classical-antiquity). It was one of the loftiest and most [sublime](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/sublime) philosophies in the record of Western civilization. In urging participation in human affairs, [Stoics](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Stoicism) have always believed that the goal of all inquiry is to provide a mode of conduct characterized by tranquillity of [mind](https://www.britannica.com/topic/mind) and certainty of [moral](https://www.britannica.com/topic/morality) worth.
